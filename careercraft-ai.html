Project Overview

Making AI feel approachable.

CareerCraft AI is an AI-powered resume builder that generates tailored, job-specific resumes in minutes. The product had a powerful AI engine, but the onboarding flow and resume generation experience weren't communicating that value effectively.

Users were dropping off during the first 3 steps of onboarding — before they ever experienced the AI magic. The interface felt cold and complex, and the connection between "upload your resume" and "get a perfectly tailored result" wasn't clear.

My task was to redesign the onboarding flow and AI generation experience — making the process feel guided, intelligent, and fast from the first interaction.

Challenge & Solution

The onboarding problem

🔴 The Problem

Users dropped off before experiencing value

35% of new users were abandoning the flow at step 2 (uploading their resume). The onboarding asked for a lot upfront without demonstrating what they'd get in return. Loading states during AI generation felt like a black box, and error messages were technical and unhelpful. The value proposition was buried.

🟢 The Solution

Value-first, guided onboarding

I redesigned the onboarding to show a preview of the output before asking for any input — letting users see the quality of AI-generated resumes immediately. The flow was restructured into 4 clear, labelled steps with persistent progress. Loading states were transformed into "AI working" moments with live visual feedback.

🔍 Research

Usability testing + funnel analysis

Ran 3 rounds of moderated usability tests with 5 participants each, identifying that the biggest confusion was around what the AI actually does with your resume. Created user flow maps highlighting the 3 drop-off points and redesigned the information architecture around progressive trust-building.

✅ Outcome

A smoother, more confident onboarding

The redesigned flow communicated AI value earlier, reduced onboarding steps from 7 to 4, and transformed opaque loading states into branded "AI generating" moments that built anticipation. Usability test participants rated task clarity significantly higher on the new designs.

💡 Key UX Insight: Value Before Commitment

The single highest-impact change was showing a carousel of high-quality AI-generated resume examples on the landing page — before asking users to create an account or upload anything. Users who saw this preview were 60% more likely to complete the onboarding flow. Demonstrating the output quality upfront removed the biggest source of hesitation: "Will this actually be good?"

Design Process

How I approached it

01

Discover

Ran funnel analysis to identify drop-off points, and conducted 5 usability test sessions on the existing onboarding flow.

02

Define

Mapped 3 user personas (recent grad, career switcher, experienced pro) and identified their specific anxieties around AI-generated content.

03

Design

Rapid lo-fi wireframes tested with 5 users, iterated 3 times before moving to high-fidelity screens and a clickable Figma prototype.

04

Test & Iterate

Two rounds of usability testing on the new designs, with specific focus on the AI loading state and the resume preview experience.

Key Learnings

What this project taught me

01

AI UX needs transparency, not mystery. Users trust AI more when they can see what it's doing and why. Transforming the loading state from a spinner into a "reviewing your experience, matching to job requirements, optimising keywords" sequence increased perceived quality and reduced anxiety.

02

Test early, test often — even on rough wireframes. Some of the most valuable insights came from testing lo-fi paper-equivalent wireframes, not polished screens. Users could focus on the flow rather than the visual design, giving purer feedback about the experience.

03

Onboarding is a product's most important UX challenge. You only get one chance to show a new user your product's value. Every unnecessary step, every confusing label, every moment of hesitation is a user you've lost — probably forever. Every element must earn its place in the flow.
